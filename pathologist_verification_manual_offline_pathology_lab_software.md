# PATHOLOGIST VERIFICATION MANUAL
## Offline Pathology Lab Software

**Audience:** Pathologists, Authorized Verifiers  
**Purpose:** To ensure safe, compliant review and finalization of laboratory reports.

---

## 1. Introduction

Pathologist verification is the **final clinical authority** in the system. Once finalized, reports:
- Become legally binding
- Cannot be edited

This manual explains how to verify results safely.

---

## 2. Pathologist Role Boundaries

### What Pathologists CAN Do
- Review entered results
- Verify and finalize reports
- Add interpretations and remarks

### What Pathologists CANNOT Do
- Delete historical reports
- Bypass critical alerts
- Disable audit logs

---

## 3. Verification Workflow

1. Go to **Results → Pending Verification**
2. Open patient report
3. Review all parameters
4. Check abnormal and critical flags
5. Review technician comments

---

## 4. Critical Value Review

For critical values:
- Confirm result plausibility
- Ensure acknowledgment exists
- Confirm escalation if required

Finalization is blocked if critical protocol is incomplete.

---

## 5. Adding Interpretation

- Use clear, concise language
- Avoid speculative diagnosis
- Use standard lab terminology

Interpretations are audit-logged.

---

## 6. Report Preview & Finalization

- Always preview report
- Confirm patient details
- Confirm reference ranges
- Re-enter password to finalize

Finalized reports are locked.

---

## 7. Common Errors to Avoid

- Finalizing without full review
- Ignoring delta warnings
- Overwriting technician notes

---

## 8. Legal & Audit Considerations

- Your username is permanently linked to the report
- All actions are timestamped
- Reports may be reviewed during audits

---
